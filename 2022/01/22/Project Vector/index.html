双语报 第 12 期

  • get...back on track

I tried to get my life back on track after my divorce.

  • get to the point where...= reach a stage where

Usually, a farmer will get to the point where financially, what they're doing is not working.

  • back and forth= backward and forward

He paced back and forth.

  • marvel

    n.令人惊异的人（或事）；奇迹

    • a wonderful and surprising person or thing

      the marvels of nature/technology

    • wonderful results or things that have been achieved

      The doctors have done marvels for her.

    • ~ (at sth.) to be very surprised or impressed by sth.

      Everyone marveled at his courage.

  • exclaimv.（由于强烈的情感或痛苦而）惊叫，呼喊

    vto say sth suddenly and loudly, especially because of strong emotion or pain

    ‘It isn't fair!’, he exclaimed angrily.

  • get about四处走动

    move around;move from place to place

    From then on,I started learning to get about with a cane.

  • tuitionn.教学，讲授，指导；学费

    the act of teaching sth., especially to one person or to people in small groups;the money that you pay to be taught, especially in a college or university

  • supervise v.监督；管理；指导；主管

    to be in charge of sb/sth and make sure that everything is done correctly, safely, etc.

  • recompose v.改组；重写；重新安排；使恢复镇静

    compose again or differently

    compose v.组成，构成（一个整体）;作曲；创作（音乐）;撰写（信函、讲稿、诗歌等）;使镇静；使平静

    • to combine together to form a whole:

      Ten men compose the committee.

    • to write music

      Mozart composed his last opera shortly before he died.

    • to write a letter, etc. usually with a lot of care and thought:

      a letter/speech/poem

    • to manage to control your feelings or expression

      I was so confused that I could hardly compose my thoughts.

  • customary adj.习俗的；习惯的；（某人）特有的，独特的，典型的

  • novel n.长篇小说 adj.新颖的；与众不同的；珍奇的

    • a story long enough to fill a complete book, in which the characters and events are usually imaginary

      to write/publish/read a novel

    • different from anything known before; new, interesting and often seeming slightly strange

      a novel feature

  • in light of 根据；鉴于；从……观点

    Ripley's thesis about sports falls apart in light of this fact.

  • numerous adj.众多的；许多的

    existing in large numbers

    He has been late on numerous occasions.

  • youngster n.年轻人；少年；儿童

    a young person or a child

    The camp is for youngsters aged 8 to 14.

  • come down to 主要涉及到

    If a problem, decision, or question comes down to a particular thing, that thing is the most important factor involved.

    The problem comes down to money.

  • cognitive adj.认知的；感知的；认识的

    connected with mental processes of understanding

  • likelihood n.可能；可能性

    the chance of sth happening; how likely sth is to happen

    In all likelihood (= very probably) the meeting will be cancelled.

    The likelihood is that (= it is likely that) unemployment figures will continue to fall.

  • endure v. 忍耐；忍受；持续；持久

    • to experience and deal with sth that is painful or unpleasant, especially without complaining

      He can't endure being defeated. =He can't endure to be defeated.

      She could not endure the thought of parting.

    • to continue to exist for a long time

      a success that will endure

  • territory n.领土；版图；领地；（个人、群体、动物等占据的）领域，管区，地盘；

    land that is under the control of a particular country or ruler;an area that one person, group, animal, etc. considers as their own and defends against others who try to enter it

  • running nose 流鼻涕

    I have watery eyes and a running nose.

  • round-trip 往返的

    A round-trip ticket is a ticket for a train, bus, or plane that allows you to travel to a particular place and then back again.

  • tax credit 免税额度

    A tax credit is an amount of money on which you do not have to pay tax.

  • file n.文件夹；卷宗；文件；档案 v.把（文件等）归档；提起（诉讼）；提出（申请）；送交（备案）；发送（报道给报社）；排成一行行走

    • ~ (on sb)a file and the information it contains, for example about a particular person or subject
    • ~ sth (away)to put and keep documents, etc. in a particular place and in a particular order so that you can find them easily; to put a document into a file
    • ~ (for sth)to present sth so that it can be officially recorded and dealt with
    • to send a report or a story to your employer
    • to walk in a line of people, one after the other, in a particular direction
    • (in) single file = (in) one line, one behind the other 一路纵队；单行

  • shame n.羞耻；羞愧；惭愧；羞耻心；羞愧感；令人惋惜的事；让人遗憾的事；耻辱；丢脸 v.使羞愧（或惭愧）；使蒙受耻辱；使丢脸

    • the feelings of sadness, embarrassment and guilt that you have when you know that sth you have done is wrong or stupid

      To my shame (= I feel shame that) I refused to listen to her side of the story.

    • the ability to feel shame at sth you have done

      Have you no shame?

    • used to say that sth is a cause for feeling sad or disappointed

      It's a shame about Tim, isn't it? 蒂姆的事让人遗憾，你说是不是？

      It's a shame that she wasn't here to see it. 真可惜她不能在这儿亲眼看看。

      It would be a crying shame (= a great shame) not to take them up on the offer. 要是不接受他们的提议，将来后悔都来不及。

    • the loss of respect that is caused when you do sth wrong or stupid

      There is no shame in wanting to be successful. 有抱负不是什么丢脸的事。

      She felt that her failure would bring shame on her family. 她觉得她的失败会使家人蒙羞。

    • put sb/sth to shame to be much better than sb/sth 大大胜过；使相形见绌；使自愧不如

      Their presentation put ours to shame. 他们的演出使我们的相形见绌

    • to make sb feel ashamed

      His generosity shamed them all.
